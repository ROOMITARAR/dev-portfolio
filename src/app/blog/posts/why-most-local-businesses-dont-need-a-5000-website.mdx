I see it happen all the time. A local service business—like a barbershop, salon, florist, or plumbing company—wants a website. They reach out to an agency, and they get quoted $5,000 or more. The agency pitches them a massive custom system, a complex content management setup, and a dozen page templates they will never use. 

Most of the time, this is complete overkill. 

If you run a local service business, your customer isn't visiting your site to read 20 pages of blog posts or log into a complex client portal. They want to find three things as quickly as possible:
1. What time you are open
2. Where you are located
3. How to book an appointment or contact you

They also want to see some pictures of your work and know that other people trust you. That means you need your phone number, address, business hours, a photo gallery, and some Google reviews embedded on the page.

That is it. 

When a site is bloated with complex code, it loads slowly. Slow load times hurt your SEO and annoy potential customers who are looking you up on their phones while parked down the street. If your page takes five seconds to load, they will click back to search results and choose your competitor instead.
